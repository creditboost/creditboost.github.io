Chase Freedom Unlimited vs. Discover It Cash Back

Home / Blog / Blog Details

When it comes to cash-back credit cards, two heavyweights dominate the conversation: Chase Freedom Unlimited® and Discover it® Cash Back. Both offer enticing rewards, no annual fees, and perks tailored to everyday spending. But which one is right for you in today’s fast-changing financial landscape?

From inflation concerns to the rise of digital wallets, credit card users are more strategic than ever. Let’s break down these two popular cards and see how they stack up in 2024.

Rewards Structure: How Do They Compare?

Chase Freedom Unlimited®

  • 1.5% cash back on all purchases (no categories, no limits).
  • Bonus categories:
    • 3% back on dining (including delivery).
    • 3% back at drugstores.
    • 5% back on travel booked through Chase Ultimate Rewards®.

This card is ideal for those who want simplicity with occasional boosts in key spending areas.

Discover it® Cash Back

  • 5% cash back on rotating quarterly categories (up to $1,500 per quarter, then 1%).
  • 1% back on all other purchases.

Discover’s rotating categories (e.g., gas stations, grocery stores, Amazon) make it a strong choice for budget-conscious shoppers who can maximize bonus rewards.

Winner? If you love optimizing spending, Discover it® Cash Back takes the lead. If you prefer steady rewards without tracking categories, Chase Freedom Unlimited® is better.

Sign-Up Bonuses: Who Offers More Value?

Chase Freedom Unlimited®

  • $200 bonus after spending $500 in the first 3 months.
  • Additional perks:
    • 0% intro APR for 15 months on purchases and balance transfers (then 20.49%–29.24% variable).

Discover it® Cash Back

  • Cashback Match™: Discover doubles all cash back earned in the first year (no limit).
  • 0% intro APR for 15 months on purchases and balance transfers (then 17.24%–28.24% variable).

Winner? If you spend heavily in the first year, Discover’s Cashback Match™ could be worth hundreds more than Chase’s flat $200.

Global Acceptance & Digital Wallet Compatibility

In an era where contactless payments dominate, global usability matters.

  • Chase Freedom Unlimited®:

    • Runs on Visa, accepted nearly everywhere worldwide.
    • Works seamlessly with Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Samsung Pay.

  • Discover it® Cash Back:

    • Runs on Discover, which has limited acceptance abroad (though improving).
    • Supports Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Samsung Pay domestically.

Winner? Chase Freedom Unlimited® wins for international travelers.

Inflation & Everyday Spending: Which Card Helps More?

With rising costs in groceries, gas, and dining, maximizing rewards is crucial.

  • Discover it® Cash Back shines when its 5% categories align with inflation-hit sectors (e.g., gas, groceries, wholesale clubs).
  • Chase Freedom Unlimited® offers consistent 3% back on dining and drugstores, which helps if you frequently eat out or buy prescriptions.

Pro Tip: Pair Chase Freedom Unlimited® with a Chase Sapphire® card to boost rewards via Ultimate Rewards® transfers.

Customer Service & Fraud Protection

Both cards offer $0 fraud liability and free FICO® scores, but:
- Discover is renowned for 24/7 U.S.-based customer service.
- Chase provides strong mobile app features, including instant spending notifications.

Winner? Discover for service, Chase for tech-savvy users.

Final Verdict: Which Should You Choose?

  • Pick Discover it® Cash Back if:

    • You maximize rotating categories.
    • You want first-year double cash back.
    • You mostly spend domestically.

  • Pick Chase Freedom Unlimited® if:

    • You prefer simple, flat-rate rewards.
    • You travel internationally.
    • You want long-term flexibility with Chase’s ecosystem.

In today’s economy, every dollar counts—so choose the card that fits your spending habits best!
